Who is Dayna, Really?

Download CV

Hey, I’m Dayna, a tenacious Kiwi with an insatiable curiosity and a knack for problem-solving. In New Zealand, we call it the Number 8 wire mentality - finding creative solutions with whatever’s on hand. Raised by an extraordinary single mother, I learned early on that resilience, kindness, and hard work pave the way for a life well-lived. She taught me that I was enough, encouraged me to chase what sets my soul on fire, and showed me the power of lifting others up along the way.

At my core, I’m a glass-half-full kind of person. Upbeat, driven, and always looking for ways to grow. Self-awareness isn’t just something I value; it’s how I move through the world. Whether it’s offering my seat on the Tube, returning my glass to the bar, or giving a stranger a genuine compliment just to brighten their day - I believe that small acts of kindness can make a big difference. To me, being a good human is about showing up, doing my part, and leaving every space (and person) a little better than I found them.

Challenge? Bring it on. I thrive on pushing boundaries, solving problems, and saying "yes" to opportunities - even the slightly terrifying ones. Growth and learning are always at the heart of what I do, and I’m constantly seeking new ways to expand my skills, perspectives, and experiences.

Engineering and problem-solving have been in my DNA since childhood. If there was an appliance in our house, you’d find me taking it apart just to see how it worked (sorry about the CD player I couldn’t get back together, Mum). My mind is constantly asking, “Is there a better way to do this?” That relentless curiosity still drives me today, whether it’s optimising systems, fine-tuning processes, or helping a team bring their vision to life.

Beyond work, you’ll find me fully embracing life’s adventures learning to drum up a storm, saving goals for Tooting Bec Reserves, snowboarding down a mountain, or simply showing up for people who need a hand. At the end of the day, it’s all about connection, impact, and making the most of every moment.

"Engineering is not just about creating technology, it's about creating meaningful solutions for the world."
